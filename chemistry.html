Spontaneity

Some spontaneous reactions (e.g evaporation of water) are endothermic, and occur when:

  • ΔS = positive such that increasing entropy is a driver of reactions
  • ΔH = negative such that energy does not need to be constantly supplied

This is because:

  • Most spontaneous reactions result in an increase in entropy
  • Increasing entropy is a driver of reactions

When one factor is unfavourable and another is favourable (i.e ΔS and ΔH have the same sign) temperature becomes the defining factor, and Gibbs free energy is used to reflect this balance between enthalpy and entropy.

Bond energies

  • Breaking bonds = releasing energy

    When breaking bonds releases more energy than the energy absorbed from forming bonds, ΔH > 0 = endothermic (+)

  • Forming bonds = absorbing energy

    When forming bonds absorbs more energy than the energy released from breaking bonds, ΔH < 0 = exothermic (–)
