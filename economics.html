Economics


Year 11 Economics - Adrian's Study Kit

  • Leakages: S + T + M

  • Injections: I + G + X

  • Factor markets: Resources which businesses use to produce goods and services

  • Opportunity cost : Benefits you miss out on when choosing one alternative over another (the alternative foregone)

  • Fiscal policy: Government expenditure and tax policies

  • Monetary policy: Interest rate and money supply

  • Short run: Some costs (e.g capital) are fixed, some costs are variable

  • Long run: All costs are variable

  • Real interest rate = nominal interest rate – inflationary expectations

  • Nominal interest rate: interest rate before taking inflation into account

  • Disposable income = gross income – taxation

  • Final income = gross income – taxation + social wage – indirect taxes

  • Aggregate demand = C + I + G + (X – M)

  • Labour force productivity = output (GDP) ÷ employed labour units

  • Autonomous investment: Investment without regard to economic growth

  • Economies of scale: Cost advantages from scaling up operations. Likewise, diseconomies would be disadvantages of scaling up operations. Internal economies of scale occur within a company's production efficiency, external economies of scale occur because of larger changes within the industry.
